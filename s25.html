ABOUT THE UNIVERSITY OF GUELPH

Established in 1964, the University of Guelph stands today as one of the top institutions in Canada offering a breadth of degrees across eight different colleges. With roots stemming back to 1874 and originally operating as an agriculture based school, the University of Guelph has spent over 150 years educating and developing the next generation with their mission to improve life. As it stands today, the university has 29,507 undergraduate and graduate students, and over 185,000 alumni from 160+ different countries across the globe.

The University of Guelph features a multitude of services dedicated to assisting and supporting students. My co-op took place within the Student Housing Services department, more specifically within the South Residence Maritime Hall office. Their main goal is to provide helpful resources to students living in residence during both the application process and existing needs throughout the school year overseeing all 16 on campus residence communities. I was employed during the summer, where the focus of the department revolves around organizing new students coming into residence for the fall semester, across 5200+ beds and accomadating a plethora of requests within the residence lottery system.

Goal 1: Learn something to improve as a developer.

My first goal consisted of learning something new in regards to the development process that can be carried over into future endeavours. Co-op provides the unique oppertunity to work on projects in a different manner than those provided in classwork. While there is overlap in how they are tackled in terms of guidelines and deadlines, working without any semblance of grading or rubric means that you need to set your own standards of completion and effectiveness. The project that took the majority of my time during the term revolved around an algorithim running simulations to find the best possible outcome. Whilst I was given no benchmark for it's performance, I realized quickly that taking an entire minute to run 100,000 seeds was horribly inefficent and that I lacked the knowledge on how properly speed things up. This lead me down a path of heavy research into optimization in C, using Callgrind to find where slowdown was occuring and what resource management needed attention. It was only through co-op that I was given the perfect chance to learn more about optimization tools and techniques, utilizing compiler options such as -Ofast and having to ensure it's optimizations did not cause complications. Even seemingly inconsequential lines of code that I would never once think to check were changed to maximize speed as much as possible. The 100,000 simulations that once took a minute to run now take a little over a second. Whilst speed is not everything it was a vital part of my skills as a developer that was inherently lacking, and watching as the time my program took slowly came down to become a mere fraction of it's original runtime has helped my ability to work towards greater efficeny for my future work.

JOB DESCRIPTION

During my time within Student Housing Services, my time was divided between three main projects. The first of these was using a Matterport Pro camera within the various residences across campus to create 3D virtual tours of rooms and units for incoming students to access online. This required myself and my colleagues to travel throughout campus and set up the camera, using the Matterport software to scan the building in 5 feet increments and ensuring that nothing went wrong in the recording process. Once the scans were complete, they could be uploaded and placed onto the University Housing website to give students an interactive look at any and all room types.

The bulk of my term was spent developing an algorithim to assign move-in times to residence rooms to optimally spread out student move in across two days. The goal was to ensure that move-in went as smoothly as possible by delegating when certain residence areas would be moving in and how many rooms would be moved into for each half hour time slot spanning from 8:30 AM to 3:30 PM. This required an excess amount of time taken to first scope out how to optimally divide the 70 different move-in groups across the two days and giving each group a number to represent it's location on campus with the closer two groups numbers being the closer together on campus they were. Once a generalized algorithim was derived to map out how many rooms should be moved into for each half hour interval, fractions of groups were placed into a time slot until every single room was provided with a time. Once the sorting was complete, a score was assigned based on the efficeny of the distribution of the current seed the algorithim used to sort. After enough simulations the best of these seeds or the 'lowest' score would be used to output a csv file detailing when every individual bed would move into residence. Whilst for time convience only ten million seeds would be checked at once, I allowed the algorithim to run for every possible seed C would recognize for the srand function (0 to 4294967295) and found that Friday had two seeds equate to a best score of 110, and Saturday had a single seed that equated to 156. Whilst these numbers appear trivial, their one in a billion nature cannot be understated.

My final main project was to create a web application using C# and .net to create a schedule for Resident Advisors, which would replace the current scheduling system which was through Microsoft Excel. Going into this project I had very little experience using the .net platform so the learning curve proved rather steep to ensure that everything was set up in a clean and orderly fashion. By using a local database, any user would be able to quickly generate a month by month schedule through an algorithim to ensure each RA had a fair and balanced amount of shifts, and easily edit any individual day. The main purpose was to not only replicate any and all functionality derrived from the Excel implementation and it's VBA scripts, but to make the process as quick and efficent as possible. Through this web portal, an entire yearly schedule could be created in a matter of seconds as opposed to having to manually input names for each and every day.
